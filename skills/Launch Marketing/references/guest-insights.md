## Anton Osika

> "For getting awareness, we've mainly been posting what we've shipped on social media, that's how people know about us."

**Tactics:**
- Share visual demos of shipped features on social media
- Leverage organic word-of-mouth through public updates

---

## Arielle Jackson (3 insights)

### Insight 1: Exclusive press launches

> "For early stage startups, we're almost always running the launch announcement as an exclusive, which means you give the news to a single outlet."

**Tactics:**
- Identify single target outlet covering companies at your stage
- Prepare website for traffic before launch announcement

### Insight 2: Bundle news hooks

> "Don't do a straight funding announcement... use that funding announcement as a news hook to tell a larger story."

**Tactics:**
- Combine funding news with product launch or partnership announcement
- Focus on making story interesting to outlet's readers, not just company

### Insight 3: Local press matters

> "Don't sleep on local press. If you have a local business or local customers..."

**Tactics:**
- Pitch local outlets highlighting how business helps local heroes or customers
- Tie company story to local trends like hiring or economic shifts

## Camille Ricketts

> "I'm a big believer in comms... there are very few incredible megaphones for what it is that you or your company is doing where you get to reach such a breadth of people with that stamp of credibility."

**Tactics:**
- Maintain straightforward, accessible relationships with press
- Leverage credible journalists for third-party endorsement of product value

## Bret Taylor (3 insights)

### Insight 1: The sizzle feature

> "[With satellite imagery] it honestly wasn't the most important part of Google Maps, but it was the sizzle to the steak and it created a viral moment."

**Tactic:** Identify the "sizzle" feature that generates buzz even if not the primary use case.

### Insight 2: Distribution > polish

> "Biz [Stone] was really focused on getting celebrities and public figures onto Twitter... we totally lost for no reason related to product at all."

**Tactic:** Prioritize distribution and "people worth following" over pure product polish in social/networked products.

### Insight 3: PLG isn't enough

> "If PLG means that you aren't actually engaging with the buyer of your software, you're not going to grow."

**Tactics:**
- Use direct sales when buyer differs from user
- Use developer-led GTM for platform products where engineers have purchasing latitude

---

## Christine Itwaru (2 insights)

### Insight 1: Readiness over status

> "There's the knowing something's coming and then there's the knowing what to do with it."

**Tactics:**
- Provide "readiness" guidance for internal teams, not just status updates
- Include specific positioning instructions with every major launch

### Insight 2: Content as definition of done

> "Treating content is a part of the development lifecycle process. You really want to treat it as a part of definition of done."

**Tactics:**
- Include in-app guides/educational content in feature development checklist
- Develop playbooks for in-app engagement with product marketing

## Christopher Lochhead

## Claire Butler (2 insights)

### Insight 1: Bottoms-up adoption

> "This bottoms-up motion is really focused on ICs... they love you so much that they're willing to put their social capital on the line."

**Tactics:**
- Focus initial adoption on practitioners (ICs) rather than executive buyers
- Enable users to leverage social capital spreading product internally

### Insight 2: The "pull" signal

> "They would take the laptop out of Dylan's hands when he would start showing it because they wanted to play with it."

**Tactics:**
- Look for "pull" signals where users physically want to interact during demos
- Launch when enough features exist to sustain full-time use for a small group

## Emilie Gerber (3 insights)

### Insight 1: Exclusive pitch formula

> "We make it very clear, 'Hey, we're reaching out with a startup launch or funding. We're offering this to you as an exclusive.'"

**Tactics:**
- Offer story as exclusive to one reporter at a time
- Include amount raised, industry space, and lead investors in initial pitch

### Insight 2: Optimize for reporter, not date

> "I highly recommend not having a firm date... if you can optimize for the reporter over the timing, you're going to see such a better outcome."

**Tactics:**
- Prioritize reporter availability over internal launch deadlines
- Avoid issuing press releases on rigid date if no coverage secured

### Insight 3: 6-week prep

> "Before an announcement happens, you probably want to kick off six weeks in advance."

**Tactics:**
- Start PR prep 6 weeks before target announcement date
- Coordinate investor quotes and media training early in process

## Emily Kramer

> "This is a marketing brief... what are the goals? What's the audience? What's the creative or unique end goal? What's going to make it different and stand out?"

**Tactics:**
- Complete GACCS brief (Goals, Audience, Creative, Channels, Schedule) for every major launch
- Share brief with product stakeholders early for buy-in on messaging and distribution

## Geoffrey Moore (2 insights)

### Insight 1: Beachhead strategy

> "The Crossing the Chasm model is, 'Can you create a viable repeatable business?' ... The answer would be if you had consolidated a market segment where you were number one."

**Tactics:**
- Focus on single beachhead segment to become the "big fish" partners want to support
- Prioritize market share in niche over broad, shallow adoption

### Insight 2: Stage-appropriate messaging

> "In the early market, the story is the technology... In the bowling alley, the playbook is 'No, where's the problem?'... In the tornado, this is when you do want to go broad."

**Tactics:**
- Use "project model" for visionaries and "solution model" for pragmatists
- Shift to horizontal sales coverage only once market enters "tornado" phase

## Karri Saarinen (2 insights)

### Insight 1: Filtered waitlist

> "We were in this private beta stage for almost a year... We invited people based on [intent signals]."

**Tactics:**
- Use waitlist survey to filter for high-intent users in target segment
- Onboard users in small cohorts to avoid duplicate feedback on same bugs

### Insight 2: Authentic founder voice

> "We wrote it more direct or authentic to us... all of us founders, we wrote our own reasons why we're doing this."

**Tactics:**
- Have each founder share personal "why" for building company during launch
- Target initial announcement at specific community where early adopters spend time

## Kevin Weil

> "We tried to get it all at once. It was a new blockchain. It was a basket of currencies... the whole world kind of went, 'Oh my God, that's a lot of change at once.'"

**Tactics:**
- Avoid launching multiple "new-to-the-world" concepts in a single release
- Introduce changes one at a time to build user and regulator trust

## Lulu Cheng Meservey (2 insights)

### Insight 1: Concentric circles

> "If you want to spread something you go out in concentric circles starting from your own desk."

**Tactics:**
- List and rank target audiences (limit 5-6) by ability to influence success
- Map where each audience "resides intellectually" (podcasts, subreddits, newsletters)
- Ensure inner circle (employees) fully aligned before moving to next circle

### Insight 2: Strategic free product

> "Give it away for free to the right people... whoever falls into that sliver of the Venn diagram, shower them with free product."

**Tactics:**
- Identify influencers at intersection of "obsessed with product" and "followed by target audience"
- Shower this "sliver" of Venn diagram with free product to generate organic evangelism

## Zoelle Egner (2 insights)

### Insight 1: PR is for credibility, not leads

> "PR is not going to get you leads or users... What it is good for is credibility, hiring, or maybe improving the response rate for your cold outbound."

**Tactics:**
- Include PR coverage in recruiting emails to increase candidate interest
- Use press mentions in cold sales outbound to establish trust
- Don't rely on PR for direct user acquisition

### Insight 2: Series of launches

> "You can have not just one big launch but a series of launches that allow you to stay top of mind, to create momentum."

**Tactics:**
- Plan series of launches every few months to stay top of mind
- Use novelty to re-engage existing communities
- Focus on momentum rather than one-time coverage
