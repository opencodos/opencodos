## Decision Tree: What Kind of Launch?

```
What are you launching?
│
├── FIRST PRODUCT LAUNCH (zero audience)
│   Priority: Build audience FIRST, delay launch
│   ├── 6+ weeks out? → Build waitlist, tease features, grow social/email
│   ├── <6 weeks? → Concentric circles: team → investors → power users → public
│   └── Key risk: Launching to silence. Fix audience before launch date.
│   → Read: Sahil Bloom, Wes Kao, Karri Saarinen insights
│
├── FIRST PRODUCT LAUNCH (existing audience)
│   Priority: Coordinated campaign across all channels simultaneously
│   ├── Have press contacts? → Exclusive to ONE reporter, optimize for their timeline
│   ├── No press? → Product Hunt (Tue-Thu) + social + email + community
│   └── Key risk: Single-channel launch. Must hit 3+ channels same day.
│   → Read: Anthony Pierri, Amanda Natividad, Emilie Gerber insights
│
├── FEATURE / MILESTONE LAUNCH
│   Priority: Compounding momentum — each launch builds on last
│   ├── Major feature? → Full campaign: blog + social + email + in-app
│   ├── Minor feature? → Social post with visual demo + changelog
│   └── Key risk: Waiting too long between launches. Ship and announce monthly.
│   → Read: Joanna Lord, Hiten Shah, Zoelle Egner insights
│
├── FUNDING ANNOUNCEMENT
│   Priority: Bundle with product story — never announce funding alone
│   ├── Have product news? → Lead with product, funding is the hook
│   ├── No product news? → Lead with vision + traction, funding validates
│   └── Key risk: "Company X raises $Y" is boring. Story must be bigger.
│   → Read: Arielle Jackson, Emilie Gerber insights
│
└── RE-LAUNCH / REBRAND
    Priority: Clear narrative on why now, what changed
    ├── B2B? → Concentric circles, direct outreach to existing users first
    ├── B2C? → Lightning strike: be unavoidable to target audience for 1-2 days
    └── Key risk: Confusing existing users. Over-communicate the "why."
    → Read: Christopher Lochhead, Marc Benioff, Kevin Weil insights
```

## Launch Brief Template (GACCS)

Before any launch, fill this out (per Emily Kramer's framework):

```markdown
## Launch Brief: [Product/Feature Name]

### Goals
- Primary goal: [Signups / Revenue / Awareness / Recruiting / Fundraising heat]
- Success metric: [Number + timeframe, e.g., "500 signups in 48 hours"]
- Secondary goal: [e.g., "Top 5 on Product Hunt"]

### Audience (rank by priority, max 5-6)
| # | Audience | Where they live | Message angle |
|---|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | [e.g., AI developers] | [Twitter, HN, Discord] | [e.g., "Ship 10x faster"] |
| 2 | | | |
| 3 | | | |

### Creative
- One-sentence pitch: [What + for whom + why now]
- Story hook: [Provocative insight that opens the pitch — NOT a feature]
- What makes this different: [One thing, not a list]
- Sizzle feature: [The viral demo moment, even if not core value]

### Channels (simultaneous activation plan)
| Channel | Asset needed | Owner | Timing |
|---------|-------------|-------|--------|
| Email to list | Launch email | | T+0 |
| Product Hunt | Listing + images | | T+0 |
| Twitter/X | Thread + visual | | T+0 |
| Blog | Announcement post | | T+0 |
| Press | Exclusive pitch | | T-7 days |
| Community | Personal post | | T+0 |

### Schedule
- T-6 weeks: Start PR prep, media training, investor quotes
- T-4 weeks: Finalize messaging, build assets
- T-1 week: Press exclusive pitched, waitlist primed
- T+0: Launch day — all channels fire simultaneously
- T+1 week: Follow-up content, user spotlights, feature deep-dives
- T+1 month: Next launch planned (compounding motion)
```

## 5 Key Steps

### 1. Build Anticipation Before Launch
Create a waitlist, tease features, and build an audience primed before launch day. Have thousands ready to act rather than starting from zero.

### 2. Tell a Story, Not List Features
Focus on transformation and problem-solving, not features. Stories about real human impact spread and stick; features are forgettable.

### 3. Coordinate Across Multiple Channels Simultaneously
A single blog post isn't a launch. Hit multiple channels at once: Product Hunt, press, social media, email, community. Creates "everywhere" perception.

### 4. Nail Product Hunt with Community and Timing
Hunt Tuesday-Thursday. Rally your community to upvote authentically. Founders engage actively in comments. Top-5 finish opens doors to press, investors, early adopters.

### 5. Treat Launch as Repeatable Motion, Not One-Time Event
Best companies launch constantly — new features, milestones, integrations, customer stories. Each launch compounds on the last.

## Top Insights

### From Sahil Bloom
*Sahil Bloom*

> "Build an audience first, then release your product to that audience."

**Key insight:** Product launches to existing email lists and social followings dramatically outperform cold launches.

**Apply this by:**
- Build email list and social following months before launch
- Launch to your existing audience first, expand from there

### From April Dunford
*April Dunford*

> "A pitch is just a story... I always want to start with insight. Why do I need to pay attention to this?"

**Key insight:** A good launch pitch has a point of view and starts with insight, not features.

**Apply this by:**
- Open every launch with a provocative insight about the market
- Frame the product as the answer to a story, not a list of capabilities

### From Anthony Pierri
*Anthony Pierri*

> "Launches are actually not just one thing, they're really a campaign... multiple things happening at the same time."

**Key insight:** A launch is a coordinated campaign across multiple channels, not a single announcement.

**Apply this by:**
- Plan 3-5 simultaneous channel activations for every launch
- Treat each launch as a campaign with pre/during/post phases
- Make launching a repeatable motion that compounds over time

### From Lulu Cheng Meservey
*Lulu Cheng Meservey*

> "If you want to spread something you go out in concentric circles starting from your own desk."

**Key insight:** Launch messaging must be aligned in sequence — internal team first, then co-founders, investors, power users, and outward.

**Apply this by:**
- List and rank 5-6 target audiences by influence
- Map where each audience lives (podcasts, subreddits, newsletters)
- Ensure inner circle is fully aligned before moving to next circle
- Give free product to influencers at the intersection of "obsessed with product" and "followed by target audience"

### From Arielle Jackson
*Arielle Jackson*

> "Don't do a straight funding announcement... use that funding announcement as a news hook to tell a larger story."

**Key insight:** Bundle news hooks (funding, partnerships, milestones) with your product story for maximum press impact.

**Apply this by:**
- Combine funding news with product availability or customer traction
- Offer stories as exclusives to one reporter at a time
- Don't sleep on local press — turn customers into heroes

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | What Happens | Who learned this |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Launching to zero audience | Silence. No signups, no press pickup, demoralized team. You get one shot at "new" — wasting it on an empty room is irreversible. | Sahil Bloom, Wes Kao |
| Single-channel launch (just a blog post) | No "everywhere" perception. Audience sees it once, scrolls past. Need 3+ channels firing simultaneously to create event gravity. | Anthony Pierri, Hiten Shah |
| Leading with features, not story | Nobody shares feature lists. People share stories and provocative insights. Features are forgettable; transformation narratives stick. | April Dunford, Lulu Cheng Meservey |
| Launching multiple new concepts at once | Libra tried new blockchain + new currency + WhatsApp integration simultaneously. Regulators panicked, users confused, launched nothing. One concept at a time. | Kevin Weil |
| Pitching multiple reporters on an "exclusive" | Reporter finds out, kills the story, blacklists you. One reporter at a time, optimized for their timeline, not yours. | Emilie Gerber |
| Rigid launch date over reporter availability | You announce on your date, get zero coverage. Reporter who would have covered you was on vacation. Optimize for reporter, not calendar. | Emilie Gerber |
| Skipping PR because "it doesn't drive signups" | True — PR doesn't drive leads directly. But it drives recruiting, cold outbound response rates, and investor credibility. Different distribution mechanism, still valuable. | Zoelle Egner |
| Waiting for "perfect" to launch | You lose the novelty window. Ship, learn, relaunch. The best companies launch constantly — perfection is the enemy of compounding momentum. | Joanna Lord, Anthony Pierri |

## Signs of Success
- Waitlist or primed audience before launch day
- Multiple channels show activity simultaneously
- People articulate why product matters in one sentence
- Each launch generates more momentum than the last
